GPS Watch vs. Rangefinder — Which Do You Need?

A rangefinder wins for exact pin distance. A GPS watch wins for hands-free pre-shot planning. Many serious weekend golfers use both. If you can only have one, get the rangefinder for accuracy.

Frequently Asked Questions

Do I need a GPS watch if I already have a rangefinder?
A GPS watch gives hands-free yardages and course layout. A rangefinder gives exact pin distance. Many golfers use both — watch for planning, rangefinder for the final shot.
Does the Garmin Approach S62 work without a phone?
Yes — the S62 has 41,000+ preloaded courses and works completely standalone. The Garmin Connect app is only needed to sync data post-round.
What is the best golf GPS watch under $200?
The Bushnell Ion Elite (~$149) is the best GPS watch under $200. Front/middle/back for 40,000+ courses, auto-hole advance, and solid battery life.
