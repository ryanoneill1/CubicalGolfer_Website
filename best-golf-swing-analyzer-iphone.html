iPhone-Specific Features to Look For

V1 Golf App compatibility

V1 Golf is the gold standard for iOS swing video analysis — used by PGA Tour instructors. Many sensors and apps integrate with V1 Golf for video overlays. If your teaching pro uses V1, look for apps that export or integrate with it.

Slow-motion video

iPhone 15 Pro shoots up to 240fps slow motion. For self-coaching, recording your swing at 240fps in the Apple Camera app and reviewing frame-by-frame is genuinely useful without any additional hardware. The Hudl Technique and Coach My Video apps are popular free options for frame-by-frame swing analysis.

Frequently Asked Questions

Do I need swing analysis hardware or can I just use my iPhone camera?

For beginners and intermediate golfers, your iPhone camera is excellent for swing analysis — especially at slow motion. Apps like Hudl Technique let you draw lines and compare swings for free. Hardware sensors add automatic tracking and shot-by-shot data that the camera alone can't provide, but the camera is a legitimate and effective tool on its own.

Is Arccos worth the annual subscription?

For golfers who play 15+ rounds per year and want to understand their game, yes. The AI Caddie recommendations alone are worth the subscription cost if they prevent 2–3 wrong club decisions per round. The strokes gained breakdown typically reveals one area of the game that deserves focused practice — which is worth more than $99 in lesson savings.

Final Recommendation

Arccos Caddie Smart Sensors at $179 + subscription is the best investment for golfers who want to understand their game from real round data. The automatic tracking and AI caddie features are genuinely useful in a way that manual analysis isn't.

For golfers already in the Garmin ecosystem, the CT10 sensors add automatic shot tracking to your existing watch setup. For the tightest budget, Shot Scope H4 at $149 delivers GPS + tracking without any ongoing subscription cost.
