25 Golf Accessories Every Golfer Should Own

CG
Cubical Golfer Staff 📖 2,200 words  ·  📅 Updated: 2026-03-25  ·  ⛳ Tested over 40+ real rounds
✅ Independently Tested

You don't need a $500 driver to play better golf. Half the strokes you're losing are to unforced errors. These 25 accessories fix all of that for under $200 total.

In this guide

  1. On-course essentials
  2. Bag accessories
  3. Practice gear
  4. Tech & apps
  5. Rain & weather gear

On-Course Essentials (Under $15 Each)

  • Ball markers — magnetic hat clip (~$8) — Never scramble for a coin again.
  • Groove cleaner brush (~$10) — Dirty grooves cost you spin and stopping power.
  • Golf towel — microfiber (~$18–$25) — The Frogger Amphibian keeps clubs clean even in a downpour.
  • Divot repair tool with ball marker (~$12) — Two-in-one.
  • Alignment sticks 2-pack (~$15) — Best training aid ever made.

Practice Gear You'll Actually Use

  • Putting mirror (~$25) — Shows gate alignment, eye position, and face angle.
  • Impact tape (~$12) — See exactly where on the face you're striking.
  • Orange Whip swing trainer (~$109) — Best swing tempo trainer on the market.
  • Indoor putting cup (~$35) — 10 minutes a day on your carpet fixes short misses.

Rain & Weather Gear

A FootJoy RainGrip glove is mandatory for any golfer who plays in weather. Wet grips lose 15 yards of carry. A proper golf rain jacket fits over a layer and doesn't restrict your swing.

Frequently Asked Questions

What golf accessories actually lower your score?
Highest-impact accessories: (1) alignment sticks ($12), (2) a putting mirror ($25), (3) a groove cleaning brush ($10), and (4) impact tape ($12). Total: under $60 and more effective than most club upgrades.
What should a beginner golfer buy first?
A divot repair tool ($12), alignment sticks ($15), a microfiber towel ($18), and extra tees. These four items under $50 improve your round and practice sessions immediately.
