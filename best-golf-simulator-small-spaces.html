What to Look for in a Small-Space Golf Simulator

Minimum ceiling height

For a full-swing driver, you need at least 8.5–9 feet of ceiling clearance. At 8 feet, you can work with short irons and mid-irons safely. The Garmin R10 and Mevo+ need room for the ball to travel at least 6 feet before hitting the screen — factor this into your setup.

Photometric vs. radar launch monitors

Photometric monitors (SkyTrak+, Uneekor) use cameras to track the ball at impact — they work in very compact spaces because they don't need ball flight clearance. Radar monitors (Mevo+, R10) need the ball to travel 6–10 feet before reading data. Photometric units generally cost more but offer greater flexibility in tight rooms.

Net quality matters more than you think

A thin, cheap net will degrade within months of regular driver use. The Net Return and similar premium nets use double-netting and reinforced seams. A net failure mid-swing is a safety issue, not just an inconvenience.

Frequently Asked Questions

What is the minimum room size for a golf simulator?

For a usable setup with a driver, aim for at least 10 feet wide × 10 feet deep × 9 feet high. You can work with less ceiling height if you limit yourself to irons. The Garmin R10 is the most forgiving radar-based unit for tight spaces.

Does SkyTrak+ work in a small garage?

Yes — the SkyTrak+ is specifically designed for small spaces because it uses photometric (camera-based) technology rather than radar. It sits beside the ball at ground level and doesn't need ball flight clearance. An 8×8 foot hitting bay is sufficient.

How much does a home golf simulator cost?

Entry-level setup (Garmin R10 + basic net + mat): $1,200–$1,500. Mid-range (Mevo+ + quality net + mat): $2,500–$3,000. Premium (SkyTrak+ + Net Return + Fiberbuilt + projector + screen): $5,000–$7,000. Most weekend golfers find the $2,500–$3,500 range delivers the best value.

Can I use a golf simulator with a standard TV?

Yes — the Garmin R10 and SkyTrak+ both connect to tablets and TVs via app. For a full projection setup, you'll need a short-throw projector and an impact screen. A 4K TV mounted on a swing arm works fine for data-focused practice.

Final Recommendation

For most weekend golfers building their first small-space simulator, the Garmin Approach R10 is the right starting point — $599 gets you real data and virtual course play without a major commitment. If you're ready to invest properly, the SkyTrak+ is the clear step-up: better accuracy, better software, and a photometric design that works in tighter spaces than any radar unit.

Whatever monitor you choose, pair it with a quality net (Net Return Pro is the benchmark) and a Fiberbuilt mat. The monitor is the data engine — the net and mat determine how often you actually use it.
