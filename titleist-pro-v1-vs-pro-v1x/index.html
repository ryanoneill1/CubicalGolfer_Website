The Core Differences at a Glance

  • Compression — Pro V1: 87 (softer). Pro V1x: 97 (firmer). The x requires more swing speed to compress fully.
  • Flight — Pro V1: mid-high trajectory. Pro V1x: high trajectory. Golfers with naturally low ball flight benefit from the V1x.
  • Spin — Pro V1: lower long game spin. Pro V1x: higher spin across all clubs — more workability.
  • Feel — Pro V1: softer off the putter. Pro V1x: firmer, more click at impact.
  • Tour usage — Pro V1: Jordan Spieth, Patrick Cantlay. Pro V1x: Justin Thomas, Scottie Scheffler.

Which Should You Play?

Play Pro V1 if: swing speed under 100mph, high natural ball flight, prefer softer feel. Play Pro V1x if: swing speed over 100mph, naturally low ball flight, prefer firmer feel. When genuinely unsure, play Pro V1 — it is the more forgiving choice for most amateurs.

Frequently Asked Questions

Is the Pro V1 or Pro V1x better?
Neither is objectively better — they suit different swings. Pro V1 for softer feel, mid-high flight, 90-105mph. Pro V1x for firmer feel, higher flight, 95mph+. Most amateurs below 100mph perform better with the Pro V1.
Can a high handicapper use a Pro V1?
Yes, if your swing speed is over 85mph. Below that, the Pro V1 will not compress properly and you will lose distance versus a low-compression ball like the Srixon Soft Feel.
