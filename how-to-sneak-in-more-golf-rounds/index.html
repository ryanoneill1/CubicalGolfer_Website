How to Sneak In More Golf Rounds This Year

✍️ Cubical Golfer Staff 📖 1,500 words 📅 Updated March 2026

You have a job, maybe a family, and a deep need to play more golf. The average office golfer plays 14 rounds a year. With a few scheduling strategies, that number can realistically hit 25–30.

In this guide

  1. Embrace the twilight round
  2. Play faster formats
  3. Work from home = morning round
  4. The golf buddy system

Embrace the Twilight Round

Twilight rates start at 3–4pm at most courses and can drop 40–60% off peak pricing. More importantly, it takes 2.5 hours instead of 4.5 because the course is emptying out. You can play 9 holes after work on a Tuesday and be home by 7:30. Do that weekly from May through September and you've added 20 rounds to your year.

Play Faster Formats

Best ball scramble with 2–3 players moves faster than stroke play. Playing 'ready golf' saves 20–30 minutes per round. 9 holes is a full golf experience — stop feeling like it doesn't count. Many serious golfers play more 9-hole rounds than 18 because the logistics are infinitely simpler.

Work From Home = Morning Round

If you have any WFH flexibility, the 6:30am tee time is the single best golf hack available. You're done by 11, showered by 11:30, and at your desk before most of your coworkers have finished their third coffee. Public courses are empty at dawn.

The Golf Buddy System

Having 2–3 committed golf friends is more important than any gear upgrade. Build a standing tee time: same course, same day, every 2 weeks. Treat it like a meeting. Say no to other things to protect it.
