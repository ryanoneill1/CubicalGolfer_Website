What Makes a Great Hot-Weather Golf Glove

Perforated or mesh panels on the back for air circulation, moisture-wicking materials on the palm, and a snug fit that doesn't bunch when wet.

Frequently Asked Questions

What type of golf glove is best for hot weather?
Look for gloves with mesh or perforated panels on the back and moisture-wicking synthetic leather on the palm. The FootJoy WeatherSof and Titleist Players Flex are the top picks.
How often should you replace a golf glove?
Every 15–20 rounds, or when the grip starts slipping during the swing. In hot weather gloves wear out faster — keep 2–3 in your bag.
