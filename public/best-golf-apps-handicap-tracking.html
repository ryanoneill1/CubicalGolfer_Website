Do You Need GHIN or Is Any App Handicap Enough?

The USGA World Handicap System (WHS) requires your handicap to be maintained through an authorised golf association (GHIN in the US). For casual rounds with friends, any app that calculates a consistent differential is fine. For formal competitions, posting competitions, or tournament entry that requires an official handicap, you need GHIN integration.

The Grint, Arccos, 18Birdies, and Garmin Golf all support official GHIN posting through the Golf Canada/USGA API. Golf Pad supports manual differential entry.

Frequently Asked Questions

What is the best free golf handicap tracking app?

The Grint offers the most functional free handicap tracking — you get a real USGA handicap index, round history, and a community feed without paying. 18Birdies is the best free choice if you also want GPS yardages in the same app. Both are fully functional in their free tiers for most weekend golfers.

Is Arccos worth the subscription for casual golfers?

For golfers who play 15+ rounds per year and genuinely want to understand where shots are lost, yes. For casual golfers who play 6–8 rounds per year, the free tier of The Grint or 18Birdies is sufficient. The Arccos value proposition scales with how often you play.

Can I use multiple golf apps at the same time?

You can, though battery drain during a round is a practical concern. Many golfers use one app for GPS yardages (18Birdies) and a separate device for shot tracking (Arccos sensors + background app). The apps don't conflict but using both actively drains phone battery faster.

Final Recommendation

For most weekend golfers, start with The Grint (free) for handicap tracking or 18Birdies (free) for GPS + scoring + handicap in one app. Both deliver everything a recreational golfer needs at zero cost.

If you play 20+ rounds per year and want genuine game improvement data, Arccos Caddie is the step-change investment — the automatic tracking and strokes gained analytics produce insights that manual apps cannot replicate.
