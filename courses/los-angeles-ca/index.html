🟢 Public Golf Courses in Los Angeles

Rancho Park Golf Course

🏌️ public 💰 $25–$45 ⛳ 18 holes 📊 Rating: 71.0

City of LA course that hosted the 1961 Los Angeles Open on the PGA Tour. Classic layout, great value near West Hollywood.

Hansen Dam Golf Course

🏌️ public 💰 $20–$40 ⛳ 18 holes 📊 Rating: 69.3

Best value public golf in the San Fernando Valley. Good conditions for the price.

Brookside Golf Course

🏌️ public 💰 $30–$60 ⛳ 36 holes 📊 Rating: 70.9

Two courses adjacent to the Rose Bowl in Pasadena. Great value and consistently good conditions.

🔵 Semi-Private Courses Near Los Angeles

Riviera Country Club

🏌️ semi-private 💰 Member/Guest only ⛳ 18 holes 📊 Rating: 75.8

Home of the Genesis Invitational, 'The Riviera of Golf.' Host of the 1948 and 1995 US Opens. Private — only accessible via member invitation or tournament.

Palos Verdes Golf Club

🏌️ semi-private 💰 $80–$150 ⛳ 18 holes 📊 Rating: 72.4

Stunning clifftop setting above the Pacific in Palos Verdes. Semi-private with occasional public access.

Frequently Asked Questions

What are the best public golf courses in Los Angeles?
The best public golf courses in Los Angeles include: Rancho Park Golf Course, Hansen Dam Golf Course, Brookside Golf Course. All offer excellent conditions and value for weekend golfers.
What is the best time of year to play golf in Los Angeles?
The best golf season in Los Angeles is Year-round (mild climate). Plan tee times in advance during peak months — popular courses book up 2–4 weeks ahead.
How much does it cost to play golf in Los Angeles?
Public golf in Los Angeles ranges from $20–$200 depending on the course and tee time. Municipal courses run $20–$55. Semi-private courses $60–$150. Twilight rates after 3pm can cut peak pricing by 30–50%.
