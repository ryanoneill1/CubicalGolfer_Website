How to Build a Garage Golf Simulator: A Complete Guide

Everything you need to plan, budget, and build a functional golf simulator in a single-car garage — from screen selection to software setup.

By Cubical Golfer Staff·Updated March 2026·15 min read

A garage golf simulator is one of the highest-ROI investments a serious weekend golfer can make. For $3,000–$7,000, you get year-round access to virtual courses, practice data, and the ability to hit shots whenever you have 30 minutes. This guide covers the complete build process — budget, component selection, and setup.

Step 1: Assess Your Space

A single-car garage typically measures 10–12 feet wide × 18–20 feet deep × 8–10 feet high. For a functional simulator:

  • Width: 10 feet minimum. 12 feet is comfortable for a right and left-handed golfer without moving the screen.
  • Depth: 12–14 feet from screen to hitting position. The launch monitor goes behind you (radar) or beside the ball (photometric), requiring 1–2 feet of additional clearance behind you.
  • Ceiling height: 9 feet minimum for a driver swing. 8 feet is workable for irons only.

Measure your actual usable space before buying anything. A tape measure and 20 minutes will save you expensive returns.

Step 5: Projector Selection

For a garage setup, a short-throw projector reduces shadow interference — you don't want your body blocking the image while at address. Key specifications:

  • Lumens: 3,000+ for garage use (garages have ambient light even with the door closed).
  • Throw ratio: 0.5–0.8 for short-throw from 6–8 feet back. Standard throw projectors need 12+ feet of throw distance.
  • Resolution: 1080p minimum. 4K is better for a large screen but costs significantly more.

Frequently Asked Questions

Do I need a dedicated room or can I share the garage with a car?

Many golfers use a one-car garage that doubles as a simulator bay. The Net Return Pro and Carl's Place screens fold/store relatively compactly. The projector can be mounted on a swing arm. A shared-use garage is very achievable — it takes 10–15 minutes to set up and break down if the screen frame is freestanding.

What's the minimum ceiling height for a driver swing?

8.5–9 feet for most golfers. At 8 feet, you can practice irons safely. If you're tall or have a very upright swing, 9 feet is the safe minimum for driver. Measure your actual ceiling height — garage ceiling heights vary significantly.

How long does it take to build a garage simulator?

With pre-built components (Net Return frame, standard screen), a weekend is typically sufficient. Allow a day to mount the projector, hang the screen, and calibrate the launch monitor. First-time builds often take longer due to projector alignment and software configuration.

Final Recommendation

Start with the entry-level $2,000 build to validate the habit before upgrading. A Garmin R10, DIY PVC frame, Carl's Place screen, and a mid-range projector will deliver real golf simulator value at under $2,500. If you use it daily for 3 months, upgrade to the SkyTrak+ mid-range build. If you don't use it — you've lost less.

The single most impactful upgrade from entry to mid-range is the launch monitor — the SkyTrak+ delivers accuracy and software capability that fundamentally transforms the experience from "pretty good data" to "genuinely useful data."
