Public golf in New York City is more accessible than most people think. The city operates 13 public golf courses across the five boroughs, and some of the best public golf on the East Coast is within 90 minutes of Midtown.

🟢 Public Golf Courses in New York

Bethpage State Park — Black Course

🏌️ public 💰 $40–$75 ⛳ 18 holes 📊 Rating: 76.6

Legendary public course. Host of the 2002 and 2009 US Opens. Weekend tee times require overnight queueing. Worth every bit of it.

Bethpage State Park — Red Course

🏌️ public 💰 $35–$65 ⛳ 18 holes 📊 Rating: 72.7

Sister course to the Black. Slightly easier but still challenging. No overnight queue needed for weekday rounds.

Pelham Bay & Split Rock Golf Courses

🏌️ public 💰 $35–$55 ⛳ 36 holes 📊 Rating: 70.5

Two Bronx courses with excellent conditions for NYC municipal golf. 36 holes total.

Trump Golf Links at Ferry Point

🏌️ public 💰 $100–$200 ⛳ 18 holes 📊 Rating: 74.3

Jack Nicklaus design in the Bronx with views of the Whitestone Bridge. The most spectacular setting of any NYC public course.

Frequently Asked Questions

What are the best public golf courses in New York?
The best public golf courses in New York include: Bethpage State Park — Black Course, Bethpage State Park — Red Course, Pelham Bay & Split Rock Golf Courses. All offer excellent conditions and value for weekend golfers.
What is the best time of year to play golf in New York?
The best golf season in New York is April through October. Plan tee times in advance during peak months — popular courses book up 2–4 weeks ahead.
How much does it cost to play golf in New York?
Public golf in New York ranges from $20–$200 depending on the course and tee time. Municipal courses run $20–$55. Semi-private courses $60–$150. Twilight rates after 3pm can cut peak pricing by 30–50%.
