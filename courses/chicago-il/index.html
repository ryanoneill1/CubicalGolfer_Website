🟢 Public Golf Courses in Chicago

Harborside International Golf Center

🏌️ public 💰 $40–$80 ⛳ 36 holes 📊 Rating: 72.4 / 73.1

Two 18-hole championship courses on the south side. Best kept secret in Chicago public golf.

Sydney R. Marovitz Golf Course

🏌️ public 💰 $20–$45 ⛳ 9 holes 📊 Rating: 35.5

Stunning lakefront views. Short 9-hole layout perfect for a quick round after work.

Cantigny Golf

🏌️ public 💰 $50–$90 ⛳ 27 holes 📊 Rating: 71.9

27 holes in Wheaton, 30 minutes west. Beautiful parkland course, excellent conditions.

🔵 Semi-Private Courses Near Chicago

Cog Hill Golf & Country Club

🏌️ semi-private 💰 $55–$150 ⛳ 72 holes 📊 Rating: 75.3

Former home of the BMW Championship on the PGA Tour. The No. 4 course (Dubsdread) is a must-play for any serious golfer near Chicago.

Kemper Lakes Golf Club

🏌️ semi-private 💰 $80–$130 ⛳ 18 holes 📊 Rating: 73.5

Hosted the 1989 PGA Championship. 45 minutes northwest of Chicago.

Frequently Asked Questions

What are the best public golf courses in Chicago?
The best public golf courses in Chicago include: Harborside International Golf Center, Sydney R. Marovitz Golf Course, Cantigny Golf. All offer excellent conditions and value for weekend golfers.
What is the best time of year to play golf in Chicago?
The best golf season in Chicago is May through October. Plan tee times in advance during peak months — popular courses book up 2–4 weeks ahead.
How much does it cost to play golf in Chicago?
Public golf in Chicago ranges from $20–$200 depending on the course and tee time. Municipal courses run $20–$55. Semi-private courses $60–$150. Twilight rates after 3pm can cut peak pricing by 30–50%.
