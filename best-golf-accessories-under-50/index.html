Best Golf Accessories Under $50

Before you drop $400 on a new driver, spend $150 on these accessories. Each one will improve your round more reliably than a club upgrade.

Best Golf Accessories Under $15

  • Alignment sticks 2-pack (~$12) — Best training aid in golf. Check aim, ball position, and swing path every session.
  • Groove cleaning brush (~$8) — Dirty grooves cost you spin on short irons. Cheap fix that pays off every round.
  • Magnetic hat clip ball markers (~$8) — Get a 3-pack in different colors — never lose your spot on the green.

Best Golf Accessories $15–$30

  • Frogger Amphibian Golf Towel (~$18) — Wet side cleans clubs, dry side dries them — the best dual-sided design on the market.
  • Putting mirror (~$25) — 30 minutes of eye-line and alignment practice beats 2 hours of mindless putting.
  • Impact tape (~$12) — See exactly where on the face you're striking — instant feedback with zero tech.

Best Golf Accessories $30–$50

  • Eyeline Golf Edge Putting Cup (~$35) — 10 minutes on your carpet fixes yips and short misses. Narrow gate builds accuracy fast.
  • FootJoy RainGrip Gloves 2-pack (~$32) — Grips better when wet than most dry gloves. Essential for humid summer rounds.
  • Golf umbrella — Gustbuster Pro Series (~$45) — Vented canopy, wind-resistant. Survives 55mph gusts without flipping.

Frequently Asked Questions

What is the best golf training aid for a weekend golfer?
Alignment sticks (~$12) are the single best golf training aid. They're versatile, cheap, and effective at the range or at home. A putting mirror ($25) is a close second.
