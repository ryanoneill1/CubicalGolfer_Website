Golf Tips for Beginners

Starting golf is intimidating. The equipment is confusing, the etiquette is unwritten, and everyone seems to be playing a game you can barely recognize. Here's what you actually need to know.

Start With the Grip — Everything Flows From Here

The grip is the only connection between you and the club. The 'V' formed by your thumb and index finger on your top hand should point to your right shoulder. This alone helps you square the face at impact.

The Three Setup Fundamentals

  • Aim the clubface first, then your body — Most beginners set their body parallel to target and let the face go wherever. Aim the face at the target first, then set your feet parallel.
  • Ball position moves with the club — Driver: opposite lead heel. Irons: move one ball-position back per iron. Pitching wedge: center of stance.
  • Bend from the hips, not the waist — Bend forward from your hip joints until the club reaches the ground naturally.

Course Etiquette — Don't Be That Person

Don't stand in another player's line of sight. Repair divots and ball marks. Keep up with the group in front of you. Announce 'Fore!' if your ball could hit anyone.

Frequently Asked Questions

How long does it take to get good at golf?
Most beginners can play without embarrassing themselves (under 120) within 3–6 months. Breaking 100 takes 1–2 years. Breaking 90 typically takes 3–5 years for recreational players.
What should a beginner golfer practice first?
Practice in this order: (1) chipping and putting, (2) short irons — 9-iron and pitching wedge, (3) 7-iron, (4) driver last. 60% of strokes happen inside 100 yards.
